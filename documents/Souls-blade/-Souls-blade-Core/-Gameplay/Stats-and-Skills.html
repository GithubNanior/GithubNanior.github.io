Base stats


There're 9 stats representing base traits of your body, which cannot be increased by choice, as warriors for example would obviously straight up spend all skill points on strength.

Strength
This is your physical power, it may represent your strength when using one hand, your ability to move your own weight and so on.

You may "increase" your strength by using both hands when holding something, around 50% extra if both are on the hilt, and up to 100% if your two hands are apart.
Weight
The total weight of your body, armor and everything else, it affects how easy it is to move yourself or for others to move you, and affects certain actions like stomping.

Your own bodyweight only has 1/3 effect on yourself, including armor and accessories, this does not count for tools and weapons unless if you're carrying them on your shoulder.
Speed
This represents how quickly you can move, no matter how strong you are, the speed of your limbs has a threshold.

This is actually you speed when walking quickly, you can bypass it by leaping, jumping, sprinting and so on, sacrificing control for speed.
Reflex
This is for how fast you can react to events, it comes in to play when you're trying to evade an attack at close notice, or whether you can even notice an incoming attack.

You try to protect yourself by reflex if your reflex is lower than the attack's speed, in which case you don't get to decide what you do, and your moves may not be good either.
Charisma
Your physical and magical attractiveness.

Charm is countered by will, not intelligence, no matter magical or physical, as it's irrational behaviour.

If you invested in charisma as a tool of manipulation, please remember- If the one you're trying to manipulate is smart enough to see through you, and has the will to resist your charm, a beautiful face won't save your ass.
Intelligence
Intelligence represents the ability to deduce information, the player can't see everything, so the character can help as the player knows what they're thinking about.
A dumb character might end up misleading their player, though.
Intimidation
Your physical and magical ability to inflict fear passively.

Fear is countered by will, not intelligence, no matter magical or physical.
Will
This is your ability to remain in control of yourself and resist irrational behavior, both natural and magical ones.
Constitution
Determines your ability to resist sickness, poison, extreme temperatures and so on.

Derived stats


Certain stats depend on the base stats, the situation you find yourself in, whether you expected it and so on.

Movement speed
Movement speed = Lowest between Speed and Strength - Weight/3.
Your speed limit for moving with full control, formulas same or similar to this one is used for most movements of your body.

Here, Speed represents your speed if your strength is optimal for your weight, while Strength - Weapon weight is when you aren't strong enough for your weight.
  • Running will normally increase your movement speed by 50%, but it's harder for you to turn or stop in track.
  • Sprinting doubles your movement speed, it's the fastest you can move, but it's nearly impossible to turn or stop in track without .
  • Leaping and Jumping has a speed value of double your movement speed too, but obviously you can't move aside in mid air, so play it smart.
Attack speed
Attack speed = Lowest between Speed and Strength - Weapon weight.
Your attack speed for swinging with full control.

In this formula, Speed represents your speed for unarmed attacks, while Strength - Weapon weight is when your weapon is too heavy for you to swing as fast as you punch.
  • Heavy swing can let you increase your attack speed up to double, but not beyond your speed stat. You'll have trouble stopping your weapon's momentum quickly, leaving you vulnerable.
  • Lunge attacks allow you to increase your attack speed by your movement speed, they're often predictable however, or require good positioning.

Skills


Skills are bits of knowledge and techniques that may help you with specific tasks, skills can be as specific or as generalized as the player wish, so we won't list any out.

Skill points system


They can be bought with exp equivalent to the absolute value of the skill bonus +1.
For example if you have -3 stabbing, you will have to spend 4 exp points to turn it to -2.
So handicaps can be kind of a big deal.
